## TL; DR

Long-context models keep landing, and each jump reframes what we treat as in-prompt memory versus something we fetch from storage. This week I am jotting down one mental model I use when I hit limits.

## The note

When everything you need fits in the window (and in budget), in-context examples and light RAG-style augmentation can feel interchangeable in demos: paste docs, ask questions, move on. Once you push past what fits comfortably, the engineering problem splits into what belongs in the window, what lives in durable storage, and how often you refresh.

No grand thesis this Sunday — just a bookmark: **measure first, then decide whether you need more context or better retrieval.** The answer is rarely "always more tokens."
